Critically discuss the Nehruvian approach to public policy making in India

The Nehruvian approach to public policy-making in India refers to the policy framework and strategies that were adopted during the tenure of Jawaharlal Nehru as the first Prime Minister of India (1947-1964). Nehru's approach to public policy was characterized by a focus on state-led economic development, secularism, and democratic governance.

One of the key elements of the Nehruvian approach to public policy-making was the emphasis on economic development through a mixed economy model. Nehru believed that the state should play an active role in promoting economic growth and development by investing in public infrastructure, heavy industries, and strategic sectors of the economy. This approach led to the establishment of public sector enterprises in industries such as steel, oil, and mining, and the promotion of import substitution policies to reduce dependence on foreign imports.

Another key aspect of the Nehruvian approach was its commitment to secularism and social justice. Nehru believed that India's diversity required the state to adopt a policy of equal treatment of all religious and ethnic communities. This approach was reflected in policies such as the abolition of untouchability, the introduction of affirmative action programs for marginalized communities, and the adoption of a non-aligned foreign policy.

The Nehruvian approach also emphasized the importance of democratic governance and the rule of law. Nehru believed in the need for a strong, centralized state that could ensure stability and social cohesion while promoting economic development. This approach led to the adoption of a parliamentary form of government, the establishment of an independent judiciary, and the promotion of civil liberties and human rights.

However, the Nehruvian approach to public policy-making has been subject to criticism in recent years. Critics argue that the emphasis on state-led economic development resulted in a slow rate of economic growth and led to the dominance of inefficient public sector enterprises. They also argue that the commitment to secularism and social justice was not always reflected in practice, and marginalized communities continued to face discrimination and exclusion. Finally, some critics have argued that the emphasis on centralized governance led to the stifling of dissent and the suppression of democratic freedoms.

In conclusion, the Nehruvian approach to public policy-making in India had both strengths and weaknesses. Its emphasis on state-led economic development, secularism, and democratic governance had a significant impact on shaping the Indian state and society. However, its limitations in practice have been subject to criticism, and subsequent governments have adopted different approaches to public policy-making in response to these limitations.
